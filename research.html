My primary research interests are in model theory, a branch of mathematical logic with a broad range of applications. More specifically, I am interested in applications of model theory in number theory, geometry, algebra, and analysis. My current work is mostly about the Zilber-Pink conjecture and the Existential Closedness conjecture. Zilber-Pink is a far-reaching generalisation of some famous Diophantine conjectures (André-Oort, Manin-Mumford, Mordell-Lang) concerned with "special" solutions of polynomial equations. Existential Closedness is about solvability of systems of equations involving addition, multiplication, and certain "classical" functions, e.g. the complex exponential function or the modular j-function.

In the past I did research in universal algebra studying the first and second order properties of De Morgan algebras and De Morgan functions.

Research projects

University of Manchester

From 2023 to 2028 I will be working on the research project "Model theory, Diophantine geometry, and automorphic functions" supported by EPSRC Open Fellowship EP/X009823/1 and Dame Kathleen Ollerenshaw Fellowship (PI: Vahagn Aslanyan). The main aim of this project is to make progress on two major problems at the boundary between model theory, complex geometry, and number theory – the Existential Closedness and Zilber-Pink conjectures for automorphic functions together with their derivatives – and explore the connections between them.

PhD projects. I am looking for PhD students to work with me on the Existential Closedness and/or related topics. I have advertised a PhD project called "The Existential Closedness problem for exponential and automorphic functions". If you are interested in this or would like to work with me on a different topic (e.g. differential algebra), please get in touch.


University of Leeds

From 2022 to 2023 I worked on the project "Model theory, Diophantine geometry, and modular functions" supported by the Leverhulme Early Career Fellowship ECF-2022-082 (PI: Vahagn Aslanyan). This is now incorporated into the above project.


University of East Anglia

From 2019 to 2022 I was a postdoc on the project "Exponentially Algebraically Closed Fields" supported by the EPSRC Standard Grant EP/S017313/1 (PI: Jonathan Kirby). The aim of this project was to make progress on the Exopnential Algebraic Closedness conjecture.
